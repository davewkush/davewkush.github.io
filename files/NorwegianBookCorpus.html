Examples of Interest from Norwegian Book(s) 

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The following is a compilation of sentences I've come across while reading Norwegian fiction that are relevant to my linguistic obsessions (and may be of interest to you, too). So far it's mainly A'-movement phenomena (apparent wh-island and RC-island violations), though there's some other stuff thrown in the mix, too.
Original Sentence English Gloss Diagnosis Source
Det er rasjonelt å være redd for deti man ikke vet [CP hvak [ ti er tk ]]. It is rational to be scared for 'what'i one NEG know [CP whatk [ ti is tk ]]. Relativization of subject(!) from embedded object question. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 93
Tavlene ble fulle av tall og små bokstaver og noen underlige kruseduller somi Jonas ikke visste [CP hvak [ ti betydde tk ]]. Boards.def became full of numbers and small letters and other curious doodles thati Jonas NEG knew [CP whatk [ ti meant tk ]]. Relativization of subject(!) from embedded object question. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 110
Og i ettermiddag skal han på en fotballtreningi jeg ikke aner [CP hvork [ ti er tk]]. And in afternoon shall he 'to' a football-training-(session)i I NEG know [CP wherek [ ti is tk]]. Relativization of subject(!) from embedded adjunct question. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 112
Deni er jeg enig i [CP at ti er undervurdert]. Thati am I agreed in [CP that ti is underrated]. Topicalization of embedded subject after overt complementizer (that-trace). Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 141
Men detk er det jo flere [RC somi ti er tk ]. But thatk is it PART many [RC thati ti are tk ]. Topicalization out of RC. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 168
Men når en etterforsker blir tatt livet av, er det naturlig... But when a detective is taken life.def of, is it natural ... Pseudo-passivization over a definite (though idiomatic) object Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 183
[Våre kjenninger]i vet jeg jo [ hvordan [ ti ser ut ]]. Our acquaintancesi know I PART [ how [ ti looks out]] Topicalization of a subject(!) out of an embedded adjunct question Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 230
Han var iført en enkel, grå dress [RC somi ingen kan fortelle deg [CP hvordan [ du vet [CP at ti koster det hvite ut av øyet]]] He was wearing a simple gray suit [RC thati no.one can tell you [CP how [ you know [CP that ti costs the white out of the.eye]]] Long-distance(!) relativization of a subject(!) out of an embedded adjunct question. Perverse! Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 259
Den brustne, anklagende blikket. [RC Somi ti anklaget ham for å ikke ha gjort noek [RC han ikke visste [CP hvah [ ti var th]]], ... The glazed accusatory look. [RC Thati accused him of to NEG have done somethingk [RC he NEG knew [CP what tk [ ti was th]]] Long-distance(!) relativization of a subject(!) out of an embedded object question. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 404
Og deti tror du [CP han gjør ti]]? Og thati believe you [CP he does ti]]? Long-distance topicalization of an object. Snømannen, Jo Nesbø, p. 413
